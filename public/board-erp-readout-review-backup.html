Project Meridian — ERP Program Program Context

Program Context and Strategic Rationale

1 / 5

Replacing fragmented financial systems with a unified platform for US commercialization.

US commercialization of obefazimod is targeted for late 2027, requiring multi-GAAP reporting, SOX-compliant controls, and integrated finance operations across entities. Today Abivax runs two disconnected Sage instances with manual handoffs between all operational systems. That infrastructure was built for a pre-commercial company and will not support the audit, reporting, and control standards ahead.

Current Operational Gaps

Systems
No integrated financial systems
France (SA) and the US (LLC) run separate Sage 100 instances with manual handoffs between all operational systems.
Financial Close & Reporting
Manual multi-GAAP consolidation
Quarterly consolidation across French GAAP, IFRS, and US GAAP is performed manually in Excel by KPMG with no documented process.
Procure-to-Pay
Manual controls and key-person risk
Vendor onboarding, invoice approvals, and payment controls rely on manual handoffs rather than system-enforced workflows.
Controls
SOX and audit exposure
Sage cannot enforce approval workflows or generate audit evidence. SOX controls depend on individual discipline.

Project Meridian — ERP Program Organization

Delivery Model — One Builder, One PMO, Clear Ownership

4 / 5

Oracle / NetSuite

Builds and delivers

  • Owns system build, configuration, data migration, and delivery staffing
  • Accountable for technical execution and scope delivery against the implementation plan
  • Provides a named implementation lead with a defined project plan

KPMG PMO

Governs and coordinates

  • Runs weekly governance cadence, RAID management, and issue escalation
  • Provides on-the-ground coordination in France between vendor and Abivax teams
  • Forces decision closure, not just status reporting

Abivax Leadership

Decides and controls

  • Sets design priorities, key process decisions, and scope boundaries
  • Commits named Finance and IT resources into the program
  • Maintains executive control without becoming the daily project manager

Project Meridian — ERP Program Risk / Decision

Key Risks and Board Decision

5 / 5
Principal risks
Risk If unmanaged Mitigation
Weak governance
High impact		 Governance cadence exists but no one forces closure. Delays compound silently until they threaten go-live. KPMG PMO with explicit decision authority and escalation rules from day one.
Internal capacity
Medium-High		 Finance and IT are already at full operational load. ERP work competes with BAU and hidden delays emerge. Phase-based staffing plan. Named owners committed before blueprint starts in April.
Scope creep
Medium		 Phase 1 absorbs treasury redesign, FP&A uplift, and reporting automation. Program becomes ungovernable. Hard boundary: Phase 1 covers core ERP only. TMS, Trustpair, and treasury run as parallel gates. FP&A and reporting move to Phase 2.

Board decision requested

Four decisions to unlock the next phase of the ERP program.

  1. Approve the NetSuite + KPMG PMO delivery model as the recommended execution structure
  2. Authorize the Year 1 program budget of ~€1M: €650K (NetSuite) + $350K (KPMG PMO)
  3. Commit to named Finance and IT resource allocation before April mobilization
  4. Maintain January 1, 2027 as the non-negotiable go-live target

Monthly progress updates will be provided to the board, tied to milestone delivery and risk status.

The board is approving a delivery model, a budget, and a resource commitment — not just a concept. Sources: execution risk analysis, Didier risk emphasis request, pillar baseline data.
