Commercial Review Findings — 5 Issues to Challenge
Overscope Risk
License counts appear too broad — 42 general + 17 procurement + 2 specialized users
PO/procurement licensing is close to "license almost everyone." Likely misaligned with actual process roles and current volume. No named-user mapping provided.
Require role-based license matrix — named role, required capability, proposed SKU, qty rationale, keep/remove/defer per user type.
Validate Need
NSAW + NSIP add-ons require architecture justification
NetSuite Analytics Warehouse (NSAW) and Oracle Integration Cloud / NSIP presented as dedicated real-time middleware. No architecture proof provided. Simpler integration options may exist for phase 1.
Request architecture note: data flows, latency assumptions, ownership, support boundaries. Confirm NSIP is actually required for phase-1 scope before accepting.
Challenge Value
Customer Success Support package may overlap with delivery coverage
ACS (Additional Customer Support) positioned outside Ali's delivery team as de-risking support (~36 hrs/quarter). Risk of paying twice for implementation coverage already in the SOW.
Challenge overlap with delivery team scope and tie any support package to measurable outcomes, or cut entirely.
Defer
E-invoicing module — not phase-1 critical
Abivax has no/limited revenue-side invoicing urgency in phase 1. Module was included in pricing without scope confirmation.
Mark as defer or optional. Confirm with Hema/Didier before accepting in contract.
Legal Flag
ZoneCapture + ZoneReconcile are third-party vendors, not Oracle
OCR (ZoneCapture) and bank reconciliation (ZoneReconcile) presented as prebuilt NetSuite modules but provided by a separate company. Jurisdiction, data processing, and contractual model need legal confirmation — especially France/EU applicability.
Route to legal when documents arrive. Confirm vendor-of-record, contract model (reseller vs. direct), and GDPR/French law coverage.
