Project Meridian — ERP Program Program Context

Program Context and Strategic Rationale

1 / 5

Replacing fragmented financial systems with a unified platform for US commercialization.

US commercialization of obefazimod is targeted for late 2027, requiring multi-GAAP reporting, SOX-compliant controls, and integrated finance operations across entities. Today Abivax runs two disconnected Sage instances with manual handoffs between all operational systems. That infrastructure was built for a pre-commercial company and will not support the standards ahead.

Current Operational Gaps

Systems
No integrated financial systems
France (SA) and the US (LLC) run separate Sage 100 instances with manual handoffs between all operational systems.
Financial Close & Reporting
Manual multi-GAAP consolidation
Quarterly consolidation across French GAAP, IFRS, and US GAAP is performed manually in Excel by KPMG with no documented process.
Procure-to-Pay
Manual controls and key-person risk
Vendor onboarding, invoice approvals, and payment controls rely on manual handoffs rather than system-enforced workflows.
Controls
SOX and audit exposure
Sage cannot enforce approval workflows or generate audit evidence. SOX controls depend on individual discipline.

Key Milestones

Clinical
Obefazimod Phase 3
Positive induction results in Jul 2025, with maintenance top-line data expected in Q2 2026.
Commercial
US Launch — Late 2027
Finance, reporting, and controls infrastructure must be in place well before the first commercial operations begin.
ERP Mobilization
April 2026
Implementation kicks off with blueprint design, governance cadence, and dedicated workstreams across all pillars.
Go-Live
Jan 1, 2027
NetSuite becomes the system of record for all 2027 financial activity.
Current finance infrastructure was built for a pre-commercial company. The ERP program closes that gap.

Project Meridian — ERP Program Organization

Delivery Model

4 / 5

Oracle builds, CFGI oversees, Abivax controls.

The delivery model keeps build and governance with separate external parties so that no single vendor controls both. Mike Markman directs the program end-to-end, with CFGI providing independent program oversight and functional assurance. KPMG supported the ERP selection phase (RFP and vendor analysis); that engagement is now complete.
