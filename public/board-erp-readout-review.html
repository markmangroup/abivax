Scope category Included items Implication
Phase 1 core ERP scope P2P controls and workflow, GL / CoA foundation, close design, core R2R controls, core master data decisions, and minimum viable treasury handoff into ERP design. This is the mandatory backbone for a controlled January 1, 2027 go-live and stronger listing readiness.
Parallel decisions and gates TMS decision path, bank connectivity sequencing, treasury-to-GL evidence bridge, US treasury scope, Trustpair / DocuShare / Concur / payroll integration boundaries, and current-state close / consolidation documentation. These items should not bloat Phase 1 build scope, but they do need owner/date discipline now because they affect architecture and control design.
Phase 2 and broader transformation FP&A uplift, management reporting improvement, reduction of Excel-heavy workflows, close acceleration, evidence-retention automation, and broader reporting / listing-readiness automation. These are natural beneficiaries of better NetSuite data, but they should be framed as the next transformation wave rather than hidden inside Phase 1.
The first phase should establish governance, confirm ownership, and protect implementation scope. Sources: treasury briefing, R2R brief, process-flow analysis, updated board slide plan.
Board ERP Readout Risk / mitigation

Principal Risks and Board Decision

Slide 5
Risk Why it matters Mitigation
Weak implementation governance ERP projects fail when cadence exists but no one has authority to force closure. KPMG PMO with explicit authority, weekly cadence, and escalation rules.
Internal capacity overload Finance and IT teams already carry heavy operational load; overloaded teams create hidden delays. Phase-based staffing commitments and named owners before blueprint launch.
Unclear ownership across firms Ambiguity between vendor, PMO, and Abivax leadership creates duplication and decision drift. Hard RACI before mobilization and explicit role split shown to the program team.
Timeline slippage Every month of delay compresses readiness against the Jan 1, 2027 target. Monthly executive review and early escalation of issues that threaten blueprint or build timing.
Scope confusion across transformation layers If Phase 1 ERP, treasury redesign, and reporting modernization are not separated, the program will become overloaded and harder to govern. Define a hard boundary between core ERP scope, parallel decision gates, and Phase 2 transformation work.

Board decision

Approve the proposed delivery model and the execution posture for the first phase of the ERP program.

  • Approve NetSuite implementation under a KPMG-managed program governance structure
  • Require named resource commitments across Finance, IT, and program leadership
  • Maintain a clear Phase 1 scope boundary while separately sequencing adjacent transformation work
  • Monitor the program against formal risk management and escalation standards
  • Retain January 1, 2027 as the target go-live date
