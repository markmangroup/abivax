Project Meridian — ERP Program Program Context

Program Context and Strategic Rationale

1 / 5

Abivax operates across France (SA) and the United States (LLC) on manual, disconnected financial systems that do not meet public-company requirements.

US commercialization of obefazimod is targeted for late 2027. That milestone requires multi-GAAP reporting (French GAAP, IFRS, US GAAP) across both entities, SOX-compliant controls that are system-enforced rather than manual, and integrated finance operations that can support audit scrutiny at public-company scale. Current systems cannot deliver this. The ERP program builds the unified financial platform required to operate as a public company.

Current Operational Gaps

No Integrated
Systems
  • Both entities run Sage 100 as separate instances with no cross-entity integration
  • All handoffs between finance systems (Sage, DocuShare, Trustpair, Concur, Agicap) are entirely manual
  • No automated data feeds exist between any operational systems
Manual Finance
Operations
  • Quarterly consolidation (French GAAP → IFRS → SEC) executed manually in Excel by KPMG with no formal documentation
  • P2P approvals, vendor validation, and payment controls rely on manual handoffs and key individuals
  • Close procedures and reporting are not standardized across entities
Control and
Audit Exposure
  • Sage cannot enforce approval workflows or validate vendor banking
  • Audit evidence trails are inconsistent and not system-generated
  • SOX-relevant controls depend on individual discipline, not system enforcement

Project Meridian — ERP Program Organization

Delivery Model — One Builder, One PMO, Clear Ownership

4 / 5

Oracle / NetSuite

Builds and delivers

  • System build, configuration, data migration, and delivery staffing
  • Owns technical execution and scope delivery against the implementation plan
  • Provides named implementation lead and accountable project plan

KPMG PMO

Governs and coordinates

  • Weekly governance cadence, RAID management, and issue escalation
  • On-the-ground coordination in France between vendor and Abivax teams
  • Forces decision closure — not just status reporting

Abivax Leadership

Decides and controls

  • Owns design priorities, key process decisions, and scope boundaries
  • Commits named Finance and IT resources into the program
  • Stays in executive control without becoming the daily project manager

Project Meridian — ERP Program Risk / Decision

Key Risks and Board Decision

5 / 5
Principal risks
Risk If unmanaged Mitigation
Weak governance
High impact		 Cadence exists but no one forces closure. Delays compound silently until they threaten go-live. KPMG PMO with explicit decision authority and escalation rules from day one.
Internal capacity
Medium-High		 Finance and IT already at full operational load. ERP work competes with BAU — hidden delays emerge. Phase-based staffing plan. Named owners committed before blueprint starts in April.
Scope creep
Medium		 Phase 1 absorbs treasury redesign, FP&A uplift, and reporting automation. Program becomes ungovernable. Hard boundary: Phase 1 = core ERP. Parallel gates = TMS, Trustpair, treasury. Phase 2 = FP&A, reporting.

Board decision requested

Four decisions to unlock the next phase of the ERP program.

  1. Approve the NetSuite + KPMG PMO delivery model as the recommended execution structure
  2. Authorize the program budget: ~€650K Year 1 (NetSuite) + $350K (KPMG PMO)
  3. Commit to named Finance and IT resource allocation before April mobilization
  4. Maintain January 1, 2027 as the non-negotiable go-live target

Monthly progress updates will be provided to the board, tied to milestone delivery and risk status.

The board is approving a delivery model, a budget, and a resource commitment — not just a concept. Sources: execution risk analysis, Didier risk emphasis request, pillar baseline data.
