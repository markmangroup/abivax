Quick read:
- CFGI proposes a two-phase NetSuite implementation.
- Phase 1 targets the core financial foundation for a `January 1, 2027` go-live and is priced at `EUR 273,000`.
- Phase 2 covers `Order to Cash`, `Design to Build / Inventory`, later-stage migration, training delivery, and hypercare, priced at `EUR 117,000`.
- Total planned fee is `EUR 390,000`, excluding separate items called out in the proposal assumptions.

Important exclusions / assumptions:
- French on-site project manager support is separate scope.
- SOX-related SDLC work is separate scope.
- Abivax remains responsible for data extraction, cleansing, and validation.
- Third-party connector licensing and vendor-side configuration are not included.
- Travel and expenses are billed separately.

Operator read:
- The proposal is credible enough to use as commercial leverage and as a fallback path.
- Its strongest practical value is the phasing argument: `Phase 1` should stay focused on what is actually required for Jan 1, 2027.
- It does not, by itself, prove that switching implementors during board week is the right move.
