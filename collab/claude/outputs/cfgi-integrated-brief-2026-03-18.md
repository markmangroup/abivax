**Subject:** CFGI Engagement — Proposed Support Model

Ken, Jean-Arnold —

After completing our vendor evaluation process and reviewing both proposals in detail, we have determined the best path forward for the NetSuite implementation. Given the depth of NetSuite's European delivery team — including strong on-premise Paris-based resources with multi-GAAP experience relevant to our French and US structure — we are proceeding with them as the system integrator.

That said, we see a pivotal role for CFGI in this program, and frankly one that no other firm is positioned to fill in the same way. The existing SOX remediation relationship, the on-the-ground presence in Paris, and the technical NetSuite depth on Ken's side give us an opportunity to put together something more integrated and more valuable than a standard program support engagement. What I would like to explore is whether we can combine the two proposals you have put forward into a single coordinated model and contract.

The framework I have in mind is organized around three areas:

**Technical and Controls Oversight** — Drawing on Ken and Guy's NetSuite expertise, we would want CFGI present at the major design milestones with a specific mandate to ensure controls are embedded in the solution design from the start, not reviewed after the fact. The natural bridge here is CFGI's existing knowledge of our SOX remediation program. The Audit Committee and CFO will want to see a clear line between our controls remediation work and how this ERP is configured — a controls-by-design deliverable at blueprint stage would address that directly and is something only CFGI can produce credibly given the context you already have.

**Program Governance** — Walid and Youness would own the day-to-day program coordination on the ground in Paris, keeping me informed through simple, readable reporting rather than heavy governance structures. Given their existing relationships with the finance team, they are also well placed to surface issues early and manage the interpersonal dynamics that tend to slow these projects down.

**Change Management and Adoption** — The French finance team will need a structured but practical change plan to get to confident adoption by January 1. With Walid and Youness already known to the team through the SOX work, I believe this can be executed more effectively and with less friction than bringing in an outside change management firm.

What I am proposing is that you come back to me with one combined proposal and one contract covering all three areas. I would expect the pricing to reflect the narrower and more targeted scope relative to broader market alternatives I have reviewed, and I am looking for a number in the €175–225K range for the full engagement.

Given that we are targeting April mobilization, I would need to have terms agreed this week. Happy to get on a call before you respond in writing if that is easier.

Mike
