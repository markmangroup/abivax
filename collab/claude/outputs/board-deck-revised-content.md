## Slide 1: Why ERP, Why Now

**Category:** Context
**Purpose:** Establish why this program exists and why it's urgent. Do not assume prior familiarity.

### Content

Abivax currently operates on Sage (France) and manual Excel-based processes across finance, reporting, and controls. These systems were adequate for a pre-commercial biotech but are not scalable for what the company is becoming.

**Three drivers:**

1. **SEC filing readiness** — US public listing targeted for late 2027 requires auditable, system-enforced controls across all entities.
2. **SOX compliance** — Current manual controls create key-person risk, inconsistent evidence trails, and timing exposure during close and reporting.
3. **Commercialization** — Revenue operations (obefazimod commercialization) require integrated procure-to-pay, close, and reporting capabilities that manual processes cannot support.

The ERP program is a prerequisite for public-company readiness, not an optional technology upgrade.

### Visual Concept
Three-column layout:
- **Current State:** Sage + Excel, fragmented controls, manual bridge, key-person dependency
- **What's at Stake:** US listing Dec 2027, SOX readiness, commercial launch support
- **ERP Program:** Single platform, control-by-design, audit-ready operations

### Footer
*The current finance operating model does not support the company Abivax is becoming.*

## Slide 4: Delivery Model and Organization

**Category:** Operating Model
**Purpose:** Show how the program will be run. Clear role split between builder, governor, and decision-maker.

### Content

The recommended delivery model separates implementation execution from program governance:

**Oracle / NetSuite** — Builds, configures, and delivers the platform. Owns technical execution, scope delivery, and implementation staffing.

**KPMG PMO** — Runs weekly governance cadence, RAID management, issue escalation, and on-the-ground coordination in France. Forces decision closure rather than just reporting status.

**Abivax Leadership** — Retains ownership of design priorities, key process decisions, and executive-level scope control. Stays in control without becoming the daily project manager.

**Alternatives considered:**

| Option | Description | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Oracle only | Oracle self-manages delivery | Limited governance, high burden on internal team |
| **Oracle + KPMG PMO** | **Oracle builds, KPMG governs** | **Recommended — best balance of control and delivery confidence** |
| CFGI as SI | CFGI for implementation + PMO | Considered, not recommended for PMO role |
| Multi-firm overlay | All firms active, no hard split | Highest coordination risk and role confusion |

### Visual Concept
- Hero: three-lane responsibility model (Oracle | KPMG PMO | Abivax) with bullet responsibilities
- Below: compact model comparison table with recommended row highlighted

### Footer
*Separate the people who build from the people who govern. Keep Abivax leadership in design control.*

## Slide 5: Key Risks and Board Decision

**Category:** Risk / Decision
**Purpose:** Name the real risks, show mitigations, and make a concrete ask.

### Content

**Top 3 Risks:**

| Risk | What Happens If Unmanaged | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Weak governance** | Cadence exists but no one forces closure. Delays compound silently. | KPMG PMO with explicit decision authority and escalation rules from day one. |
| **Internal capacity** | Finance and IT teams already carry full operational load. ERP work competes with BAU. | Phase-based staffing plan with named owners committed before blueprint starts. |
| **Scope creep** | Phase 1 ERP absorbs treasury redesign, FP&A uplift, and reporting automation — program becomes ungovernable. | Hard boundary: Phase 1 = core ERP (P2P, GL, close, controls). Parallel gates = TMS, Trustpair, treasury. Phase 2 = FP&A, reporting automation. |

**Board decision requested:**

1. **Approve** the NetSuite + KPMG PMO delivery model
2. **Authorize** the program budget (~€650K Year 1 NetSuite + $350K KPMG PMO)
3. **Commit** to named Finance and IT resource allocation before April mobilization
4. **Maintain** January 1, 2027 as the non-negotiable go-live date

The board will receive monthly progress updates tied to milestone delivery and risk status.

### Visual Concept
- Left: risk table (3 rows — tight, not 5)
- Right: dark-accent ask box with 4 numbered decision items
- Strong visual weight on the ask box — this is where the eye should land

### Footer
*The board is being asked to approve a delivery model, a budget, and a resource commitment — not just a concept.*
